# Sample United States Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) Policy

## Overview

The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows residential and commercial property owners 
to credit a percentage of solar installation costs against their federal income taxes.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Residential (Form 5695)
- Applicant must have tax liability equal to or greater than the credit amount
- Installation must be at primary or secondary residence in the United States
- Must own (not lease) the system or have ownership agreement
- System must meet standards of the American Society of Testing and Materials (ASTM)

### Commercial (Form 3468)
- Property must be used in active business or for production of income
- Must maintain project records for verification
- System must be new property or substantially reconstructed property
- Placed in service during the tax year the credit is claimed

## Credit Rates and Schedule

### Current Rates (2024)
- **Residential**: 30% of qualifying costs
- **Commercial**: 30% of qualifying costs

### Scheduled Reductions
- 2024-2025: 30%
- 2026-2029: 26%
- 2030-2032: 22%
- 2033 and later: Credit expires (unless extended by Congress)

## Qualifying Costs

### Included in Credit Base
- Solar panels and associated mounting hardware
- Inverters (device converting DC to AC)
- Batteries for energy storage (up to 10 kWh residential)
- Electrical equipment including wiring, disconnects, panels
- Permitting and inspection fees
- Labor costs for installation
- Sales tax on components

### NOT Included
- Land or property
- Personnel and administrative costs
- Any amount financed from non-qualified loans

## Documentation Requirements

1. **Form 5695** (Residential) or **Form 3468** (Commercial)
2. Manufacturer's specifications and certifications
3. Copy of installation contract and invoice
4. Proof of payment
5. System inspection and approval documentation
6. Property ownership documentation

## Interaction with Depreciation

- For commercial systems: Must reduce basis by 50% of ITC claimed
- For residential systems: Basis reduction not applicable
- Depreciation schedule remains MACRS 5-year property

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I carry forward unused tax credit?
- **Residential**: No carryback/carryforward (expires if not used)
- **Commercial**: Can be carried back 1 year and forward 20 years

### Does the credit apply to battery storage?
- **Standalone batteries**: Not eligible (must be paired with solar)
- **Solar + battery systems**: Eligible for integrated systems
- **Capacity limit**: Residential limited to 10 kWh for battery credit

### What if I have negative income?
- **Residential**: Credit is non-refundable; limited to tax liability
- **Commercial**: Business income determines eligibility

## State Coordination

Many states offer additional incentives that stack with federal ITC:
- Check state-specific tax credit programs
- Some states require federal ITC first
- Incentive order may affect total benefit

## Energy Storage Updates (2024)

- Enhanced commercial battery depreciation
- Potential for direct payment in certain scenarios
